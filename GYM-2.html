Gym Schedule for Muscle Gain & Athletic Physique

This schedule is designed to complement your sports activities and allow for sufficient rest and recovery. It focuses on a 4-day split, targeting different muscle groups each day.

Days: Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday, Sunday

Time: Wednesday & Thursday (6-8 PM), Saturday & Sunday (Morning - adjust to your preference)

Workout Split:

  • Wednesday (Evening): Upper Body - Push
  • Exercises:
  1. Barbell Bench Press: 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
  2. Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
  3. Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
  4. Lateral Raises: 3 sets of 12-15 repetitions
  5. Triceps Dips: 3 sets to failure
  6. Overhead Triceps Extension: 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
  • Thursday (Evening): Lower Body
  • Exercises:
  1. Barbell Back Squats: 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
  2. Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
  3. Leg Press: 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
  4. Leg Extensions: 3 sets of 12-15 repetitions
  5. Hamstring Curls: 3 sets of 12-15 repetitions
  6. Calf Raises (Standing or Seated): 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions
  • Saturday (Morning): Full Body & Core
  • Exercises:
  1. Pull-ups (or Lat Pulldowns): 3 sets to failure (or 8-12 reps Lat Pulldowns)
  2. Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions per side
  3. Overhead Press: 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
  4. Goblet Squats: 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
  5. Plank: 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds
  6. Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions
  7. Leg Raises: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions
  • Sunday (Morning): Upper Body - Pull
  • Exercises:
  1. Lat Pulldowns (or Pull-ups): 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
  2. Seated Cable Rows: 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
  3. Face Pulls: 3 sets of 15-20 repetitions
  4. Barbell Bicep Curls: 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions
  5. Hammer Curls: 3 sets of 10-15 repetitions
  6. Concentration Curls: 3 sets of 12-15 repetitions

Important Considerations for your Gym Schedule:

  • Warm-up: Always start each workout with 5-10 minutes of light cardio and dynamic stretching.
  • Cool-down: Finish each workout with 5-10 minutes of static stretching.
  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increase the weight, repetitions, or sets as you get stronger. This is crucial for muscle growth.
  • Proper Form: Focus on maintaining correct form throughout each exercise to prevent injury and maximize muscle engagement. Watch videos and consider asking a gym trainer for guidance if you are unsure about form.
  • Rest: Allow for at least 60-90 seconds of rest between sets.
  • Listen to your body: If you feel pain, stop the exercise and rest. Don't push through pain.
  • Flexibility: This is a sample schedule. You can adjust exercises based on your gym equipment and personal preferences. You can also swap exercises to keep your workouts varied.
  • Cardio: Your sports activities (Tennis, Football, Badminton/Field Hockey) provide a good amount of cardio. You can add a light cardio session (like jogging or cycling for 20-30 minutes) on one of your gym days or rest days if you wish to improve cardiovascular fitness further.

Diet Plan for Muscle Gain & Athletic Physique

To gain muscle, you need to consume slightly more calories than you burn (calorie surplus) and eat enough protein to support muscle growth. Here's a sample diet plan focusing on these principles.

General Dietary Guidelines:

  • Calorie Surplus: Aim for a slight calorie surplus of around 250-500 calories per day above your maintenance level. You can use online calculators to estimate your maintenance calorie needs based on your activity level.
  • Protein Intake: Consume 1.6 - 2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of your body weight daily. For you (78 kg), this is approximately 125 - 172 grams of protein per day.
  • Carbohydrates: Choose complex carbohydrates as your primary source of energy. Good sources include brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole wheat bread, and sweet potatoes.
  • Healthy Fats: Include healthy fats in your diet from sources like avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day, especially around your workouts.
  • Meal Frequency: You can distribute your meals throughout the day as per your convenience. Aim for at least 3-4 meals including snacks.

Sample Daily Meal Plan:

  • Breakfast (around 8-9 AM):
  • Oatmeal (1 cup cooked) with berries (1/2 cup) and nuts (1/4 cup)
  • Protein shake (1 scoop whey protein with milk or water)
  • 2-3 Eggs (Omelette or scrambled) with spinach or vegetables
  • Mid-Morning Snack (around 11 AM - 12 PM):
  • Greek yogurt (1 cup) with fruit (1/2 cup) and granola (1/4 cup)
  • Handful of almonds or walnuts
  • Protein bar
  • Lunch (around 2-3 PM):
  • Chicken or fish (150-200g) grilled or baked
  • Brown rice or quinoa (1 cup cooked)
  • Vegetables (1-2 cups) - Salad or cooked vegetables
  • Pre-Workout Snack (30-60 minutes before gym - around 5-5:30 PM on Wed & Thu, before morning gym on weekends):
  • Banana with peanut butter (1-2 tablespoons)
  • Small protein shake
  • Whole wheat bread with avocado
  • Post-Workout Meal (Immediately after gym - around 7-8 PM on Wed & Thu, after morning gym on weekends):
  • Protein shake (1-2 scoops whey protein with water or milk)
  • Fruits (banana, apple, or orange)
  • Dinner (around 9-10 PM):
  • Lean meat (chicken, fish, or lean beef - 150-200g) or lentils/beans for vegetarians
  • Sweet potato or brown rice (1 cup cooked)
  • Vegetables (1-2 cups) - Salad or cooked vegetables
  • Before Bed Snack (Optional, if needed - around 11 PM):
  • Casein protein shake (slow-digesting protein)
  • Small portion of cottage cheese

Food Sources to Focus On:

  • Protein Sources: Chicken breast, fish (salmon, tuna, cod), lean beef, eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, whey protein, casein protein, lentils, beans, tofu, chickpeas.
  • Carbohydrate Sources: Brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole wheat bread, sweet potatoes, fruits, vegetables.
  • Healthy Fat Sources: Avocados, nuts (almonds, walnuts, peanuts), seeds (chia, flax, sunflower), olive oil, fatty fish.

Important Dietary Tips:

  • Meal Prep: Prepare meals in advance, especially for weekdays, to ensure you stick to your diet plan despite your busy schedule.
  • Track your calories and protein: Initially, you may want to track your calorie and protein intake using a food tracking app to ensure you are meeting your goals.
  • Adjust portions: Adjust portion sizes based on your hunger levels and progress. If you are not gaining weight, you may need to slightly increase your calorie intake.
  • Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and excessive unhealthy fats.
  • Consistency is key: Stick to your diet plan as consistently as possible for best results.
  • Supplements (Optional): Whey protein can be a convenient way to meet your protein needs, especially post-workout. Creatine monohydrate is another supplement that can help with muscle gain and strength. However, focus on whole foods first. Consult a healthcare professional or registered dietitian before starting any supplements.
